May 2026 Visa Bulletin: EB-2 India Final Action Date Holds Steady to July 15, 2014

📅 May 16, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ NRIBeat Editorial Source: travel.state.gov ↗

The U.S. Department of State has released the May 2026 Visa Bulletin. For EB-2 India, the Final Action Date holds steady to July 15, 2014. EB-3 India Final Action Date holds steady to November 15, 2013. The Dates for Filing chart has been authorized by USCIS this month.

Key Takeaway for May 2026

No movement this month — monitor the next bulletin closely.

Table A — Final Action Dates (FAD)

The Final Action Date is the hard cutoff. Your I-485 adjustment of status (or immigrant visa) can only be approved once your priority date is on or before this date and a visa number is available. This is the date that determines when your green card is actually granted.

Table B — Dates for Filing (DFF)

The Dates for Filing is an earlier date that, when USCIS authorizes it, lets you file your I-485, EAD (work permit), and Advance Parole before your Final Action Date is reached. Filing earlier means getting work authorization and travel documents sooner — even though your green card won't be approved until the FAD is current. USCIS decides each month whether to authorize this chart.

USCIS has authorized the Dates for Filing chart this month. You may be able to file your I-485 / EAD / Advance Parole using the dates below, even if the Final Action Date has not yet been reached.

The Dates for Filing for EB-2 India (January 15, 2015) is approximately 184 days ahead of the Final Action Date — meaning eligible applicants can file I-485 that much earlier and get EAD/AP while waiting for FAD to catch up.

What This Movement Means for Your Case

EB-2 India Final Action Date is advancing roughly 15–20 days per month in normal months, translating to 6–8 months of progress per year. If your priority date is within 18–24 months of the current FAD, start preparing now: ensure your I-140 is approved, I-485 medical (Form I-693) is current, and employer sponsorship is active.

EB-2 vs EB-3 India: Which Is Ahead?

This month, EB-2 India FAD (July 15, 2014) is approximately 242 days ahead of EB-3 India FAD (November 15, 2013). If you hold an advanced degree, upgrading from EB-3 to EB-2 may be worth discussing with your attorney — but category changes have significant implications for your I-140 and PERM. Never switch without comprehensive legal advice.
