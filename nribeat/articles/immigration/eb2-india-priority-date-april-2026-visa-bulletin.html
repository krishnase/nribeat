The April 2026 Visa Bulletin has been released, and here's the critical update: EB-2 India's final action priority date remains stalled at July 15, 2014—a sign that India's employment-based green card backlog continues to strain the system. If you're an H1B visa holder waiting for your green card, or monitoring your EB-2 India priority date, this bulletin reveals crucial information about your timeline and next steps.

What You Need to Know

  • EB-2 India Final Action Priority Date: July 15, 2014 (no movement from previous bulletin)
  • EB-2 India Filing Date: January 15, 2015 (allows I-140 applications)
  • EB-1 India Priority Date: April 1, 2023 (significantly ahead of EB-2)
  • EB-3 India Priority Date: November 15, 2013 (moving slower than EB-2)
  • What This Means: Indian EB-2 applicants with priority dates before July 15, 2014 may be approaching visa availability; those after this date face a 11+ year wait

EB-2 India Priority Date: Understanding the April 2026 Stagnation

The April 2026 Visa Bulletin shows that EB-2 India's final action priority date has not advanced—it remains frozen at July 15, 2014. This is critical information for the hundreds of thousands of Indian professionals on H1B visas waiting for green card processing.

For context: if your priority date is July 16, 2014 or later, your case is not yet current. The difference between final action and filing dates is important. While the final action date (July 15, 2014) determines who can receive a green card, the filing date (January 15, 2015) tells you whether you can submit your I-485 application or adjust status if you're already in the United States.

The stagnation reflects India's massive employment-based green card backlog—driven by per-country visa limits of 7% of the total pool and India's large workforce applying for EB-2 and EB-3 categories. Unlike the H1B visa 2025 lottery results that brought excitement to many applicants, green card processing for India remains a marathon.

EB-1 vs. EB-2 vs. EB-3: Which Category Is Moving Fastest in 2026?

The April 2026 bulletin reveals a stark contrast in processing speeds across employment-based categories for Indian applicants:

  • EB-1 (Extraordinary Ability/Managers): Priority date at April 1, 2023—the fastest movement. If you qualify under EB-1, you're looking at roughly 2-3 years from priority date to green card.
  • EB-2 (Advanced Degree/Professionals): Priority date at July 15, 2014—slowest, with applicants facing 11+ year waits from priority date to current status.
  • EB-3 (Skilled Workers): Priority date at November 15, 2013—marginally slower than EB-2, despite being a less-preferred category. This is due to per-country limits affecting both categories equally.

Why the disparity? EB-1 has no per-country limits and lower demand, making it the fastest path for eligible applicants. EB-2 and EB-3 are constrained by the same per-country visa cap, but EB-2 sees more demand from Indian professionals with master's degrees.

What This Means for You

Your next steps depend entirely on your priority date and current visa status:

If your EB-2 India priority date is before July 15, 2014: You're approaching current status. Monitor the May 2026 bulletin closely. You may be able to file your I-485 application (if not already done) and move toward adjustment of status or consular processing.

If your EB-2 India priority date is July 16, 2014 or later: Your green card processing timeline stretches years into the future. Consider whether an EB-1 re-evaluation is possible, or explore options like premium processing for your I-140 to secure your priority date earlier.

If you're on H1B visa awaiting green card processing in 2025-2026: The green card processing time for India remains your biggest challenge. Plan for extended H1B renewals and consider visa stamping or consular processing strategies depending on whether you're in the US or abroad.

Step-by-Step Guide: What to Do After the April 2026 Bulletin Release

Step 1: Locate Your Priority Date

Check your I-140 approval notice or USCIS receipt number. Your priority date is the date USCIS received your PERM labor certification application or I-140 petition, depending on your category.

Step 2: Compare with the Visa Bulletin

If your priority date is on or before July 15, 2014 (for EB-2 India), your case is current as of April 2026. If it's after, calculate the approximate wait: typically 11+ years from priority date to current status for EB-2 India.

Step 3: Determine Your Next Action

  • If current: File or update your I-485 application immediately
  • If not current: Monitor monthly bulletins and plan visa renewal/status maintenance
  • Consider: Consulting an immigration attorney about EB-1 re-evaluation or other options

Step 4: Plan for H1B Renewal and Status

If you're on H1B while waiting for green card processing, file your H1B extension well before expiration. Many Indian professionals renew H1B visas 3-4 times before green card approval becomes current.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does EB-2 India priority date July 15, 2014 mean for my application?

If your priority date is on or before July 15, 2014, your case is current as of April 2026, meaning you can pursue green card processing through I-485 adjustment or consular processing. If your priority date is after this date, your case is not yet current, and you must wait for the priority date to advance in future visa bulletins. Based on historical trends, this could take several more years.

How long does EB-2 India green card processing take from priority date to approval in 2026?

As of 2026, the total green card processing time for EB-2 India from priority date to current status averages 11+ years. Once your priority date becomes current, I-485 processing itself typically takes 6-12 months. This means Indian EB-2 applicants should expect a 12-13 year journey from initial PERM filing to green card approval.

Can I speed up my EB-2 India green card if I have an H1B visa 2025 extension approved?

H1B visa status does not speed up green card processing timelines. However, having valid H1B status allows you to remain in the US while your priority date advances. You cannot use premium processing to expedite EB-2, but you can explore EB-1 re-evaluation, filing I-485 as soon as your priority date becomes current, or consulting an immigration attorney about alternative employment-based categories.

Conclusion

The April 2026 Visa Bulletin confirms that EB-2 India remains in backlog, with no movement in the final action priority date. For those with later priority dates, prepare for a long-term immigration journey while maintaining H1B status.
