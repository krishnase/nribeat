EB-2 India May 2026 Visa Bulletin Prediction: Modest Progress Expected

Current Status (April 2026): EB-2 India Final Action Date stands at July 15, 2014, representing an 11-year-plus backlog for Indian applicants.

May 2026 Prediction Summary

Based on comprehensive historical analysis, seasonal patterns, and recent movement trends, we forecast the May 2026 EB-2 India Final Action Date will advance to approximately July 25 to August 10, 2014—representing 10-26 days forward movement.

Reasoning & Analytical Framework

1. Seasonal Context

May falls within the April-June window, historically characterized by steady but moderate movement. This period typically sees:

  • Continued consumption of FY 2026 visa numbers (allocated in October)
  • No fiscal year reset to create surge demand
  • Processing rates normalized after Q1 activity
  • Average historical movement: 10-14 days/month

2. Recent Movement Analysis

The last 6 months show a distinctive pattern: [304, 14, 16, 14, 14, 14]

  • October anomaly: The 304-day jump represents the FY start surge—an outlier event that occurs annually
  • November-April trend: Normalized to 14-16 days/month consistently
  • Stabilization signal: This steady 14-day pattern suggests processing has reached equilibrium

3. 12-Month Average Context

EB-2 India averaged 72.7 days/month (positive months only), but this includes the October FY spike. Excluding outliers, the realistic expectation is significantly lower. The recent 6-month pattern of 14-16 days represents the true normalized rate.

4. Visa Number Availability

FY 2026 (Oct 2025 - Sept 2026) annual employment-based visa allocation: ~140,000 across all categories and countries. EB-2 India typically consumes approximately 7-9% of total EB visas. By May, we're in month 8 of the fiscal year—approximately 67% of the annual quota should be allocated, leaving sufficient numbers for normal processing.

5. No Retrogression Indicators

Retrogression occurs when visa demand exceeds supply. Current signals indicate:

  • Steady, consistent monthly movement (not declining)
  • No pattern of stalled dates (which precedes retrogression)
  • Months until fiscal year-end: 5 months remaining in FY 2026
  • Processing pace sustainable through September

What Could Change This Prediction?

Upside Drivers:

  • Higher-than-expected visa number allocation for EB-2
  • Faster processing due to administrative improvements
  • Lower demand from other countries in EB-2

Downside Risks:

  • Sudden surge in EB-2 India demand (new batch of I-485 approvals)
  • Administrative processing backlogs
  • Unexpected policy changes

Retrogression Risk Assessment for EB-2 India

May 2026 Retrogression Risk: LOW (8-12% probability)

Timeline before potential retrogression: 4-5 months minimum (likely July-September 2026)

Reasoning:

  • Consistent forward movement observed every month for 6+ months
  • No stagnation or backward movement signals
  • Sufficient time remaining in FY 2026 (5 months) to accommodate queue
  • Historical retrogression patterns show decline in movement BEFORE reversal occurs—we haven't entered that decline phase
  • EB-3 India (earlier cutoff: Nov 2013) also shows positive movement, suggesting visa numbers are adequate

When to Watch for Retrogression:

  • July-August 2026: Monitor for movement slowdown (first warning sign)
  • September 2026: Highest historical retrogression risk (fiscal year runs dry)
  • If May movement is <8 days, increase concern to MEDIUM risk

Impact on Your Green Card Timeline

If you filed I-485 in 2014 (EB-2 India): You're approaching visa number assignment. This modest forward movement keeps the trajectory steady. Expect priority date approval 12-18 months from now.

If you filed I-485 in 2015-2016: You're still 1-2+ years away. Current pace suggests your approval in 2027-2028.

If you filed I-485 in 2017+: Long-term applicants should monitor September 2026 and October 2026 (FY 2027 start) carefully for any retrogression.

Analyst Notes

The consistency of the 14-16 day monthly movement is the most reliable predictor here. While the October spike is dramatic, the subsequent pattern is remarkably stable—suggesting USCIS has normalized processing to match available visa numbers. This stability is actually a positive sign, indicating the system is in equilibrium rather than under stress.

The key date to watch will be July 2026. Historically, fiscal years tighten in months 10-12 (July-September). If movement suddenly drops below 5 days in July or shows any backward movement, that's your signal to prepare for potential retrogression risk heading into FY 2027.
