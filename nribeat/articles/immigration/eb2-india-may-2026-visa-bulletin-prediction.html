Reasoning Behind the Forecast

Our prediction model incorporates multiple data streams:

1. Seasonal Context (Critical Factor)

May is positioned in the April-June quarterly window, historically characterized by steady but moderate movement. This is the tail end of FY2026, before the July-September retrogression risk period. Consulates and USCIS typically process cases methodically without dramatic acceleration, as visa number allocation stabilizes after the October surge.

2. Recent Momentum Analysis

The last 6 months show: [304, 14, 16, 14, 14, 14] days of movement. The 304-day jump likely represents a bulletin adjustment or catchup from February. Since then, movement has stabilized at 14-16 days monthly. This represents the "normalized" rate we should expect in May.

3. Visa Demand vs. Supply Dynamics

EB-2 India currently sits at July 2014 (11+ year wait). With approximately 40,000 annual EB-2 visa allocations and significant cross-chargeability from other countries, India's backlog absorption remains gradual. May typically sees 12-18 days of movement as agencies process without acceleration pressure.

4. Comparative EB-3 Trajectory

EB-3 India at November 2013 (12+ year wait) with 43.4 days/month average suggests EB-2 should move slightly faster due to higher visa allocation. Our 12-18 day estimate aligns with this differential.

Retrogression Risk Assessment: LOW (5% probability)

Why retrogression is unlikely in May 2026:

  • Timing within fiscal year: May is Month 8 of FY2026. Retrogression typically occurs July-September (months 10-12) when visa numbers deplete. May still has adequate allocation remaining.
  • EB-2 category strength: EB-2 is the preferred employment-based category with robust visa allocation. Unlike EB-3, it rarely retrogresses mid-year.
  • Historical precedent: In the past 12 months, no retrogression occurred during April-June months.
  • India's allocation: India receives approximately 10% of EB-2 visas, translating to roughly 4,000 visas monthly across all priority dates.

Retrogression becomes material risk starting July 2026 (FY2027 begins October 2026, but July-Sept visa exhaustion can trigger cutoffs).

What Could Change This Prediction?

Upside Catalysts (Faster Movement)

  • Administrative processing clearances: If I-485 approvals accelerate due to policy changes, 20-25 day movement possible.
  • Visa number redistribution: If other employment categories under-utilize their allocation, spillover to EB-2 could occur (+5-8 days).
  • Employment demand surge: Unexpected immigration policy changes favoring employment-based green cards.

Downside Risks (Slower Movement)

  • Staffing shortages: USCIS field office capacity constraints could reduce processing to 8-10 days.
  • Early fiscal year pressure: If FY2027 visa numbers are lower than expected, conservative cutoff policies may emerge in May.
  • Priority date backlog surge: Unexpected influx of I-140 approvals from prior years creating processing bottlenecks.

Implications for EB-2 India Applicants

If You Have Priority Date: July 2014 or Earlier

May 2026 Visa Bulletin could bring you within 1-2 months of current date. Prepare your adjustment of status or consular processing documentation now. Monitor USCIS receipt numbers closely in early June.

If You Have Priority Date: August 2014 - December 2014

You are 1-5 months away from visa availability. Begin medical examinations and background check preparation. File I-485 if eligible under concurrent filing provisions.

If You Have Priority Date: 2015 or Later

Current trajectory suggests 14-15 days/month movement = approximately 170-180 days/year advancement. At this rate, 2015 applicants face 8-10 month waits; 2020 applicants face 4-5 year waits. This underscores the importance of EB-1C alternative pathways or employment changes if available.
