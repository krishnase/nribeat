March 2026 Visa Bulletin: EB-2 India Priority Date Update

The March 2026 Visa Bulletin from the U.S. Department of State brings modest movement in employment-based green card priority dates for Indian professionals. The EB-2 India final priority date has advanced to September 15, 2013, while EB-2 filing dates moved to November 1, 2014. If you're an H1B holder, OPT student, or green card applicant from India, understanding these priority date shifts is critical to planning your immigration timeline and knowing when you may be eligible to file Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status). This article breaks down what the latest visa bulletin means for your green card journey.

What You Need to Know

  • EB-2 Final Priority Date (India): Advanced to September 15, 2013 — a modest forward movement in March 2026
  • EB-2 Filing Date (India): Now at November 1, 2014, allowing earlier I-485 applications for some applicants
  • EB-3 Priority Dates: Final date at November 15, 2013; filing date at August 15, 2014
  • EB-1 Movement: EB-1 final priority date remains at March 1, 2023, reflecting continued visa demand across all employment categories
  • What This Means: Thousands of Indian professionals with earlier priority dates can now proceed with green card applications, reducing USCIS processing time delays

Understanding the March 2026 Priority Date Movement

Each month, USCIS and the State Department release the Visa Bulletin, which determines when applicants can file for adjustment of status or consular processing. Priority dates measure your place in the green card queue—the earlier your priority date, the sooner you may become eligible to apply for permanent residency.

In March 2026, EB-2 India priority dates moved forward by approximately one month. While this may seem incremental, it's significant for Indian professionals. EB-2 has historically faced the longest backlogs due to the per-country limit (7% of all employment-based visas), which disproportionately impacts Indian nationals.

Why Priority Dates Matter for Indian Applicants

India faces exceptional demand in the EB-2 and EB-3 categories, with over 1.4 million pending cases in the national visa backlog as of 2026. Because employment-based visas are allocated per-country (rather than globally), Indian professionals often experience waits of 10+ years. Understanding the current priority date directly tells you:

  • Whether you can file Form I-485 with USCIS now
  • Your estimated timeline to green card approval
  • Whether you can renew H1B visas while waiting (typically yes, with certain conditions)
  • Your eligibility for AC21 job changes without restarting the green card process

EB-2 vs. EB-3: What's the Difference?

Many Indian professionals wonder whether to pursue EB-2 or EB-3 sponsorship. The March 2026 visa bulletin clarifies the trade-offs:

EB-2 (Priority Date: Sept 15, 2013) requires an advanced degree or exceptional ability. The priority date is older (further back in the queue), but the category is generally preferred by employers and professionals aiming for faster approval timelines.

EB-3 (Priority Date: Nov 15, 2013) requires a bachelor's degree or skilled work experience. The priority date is slightly newer than EB-2, but both face significant visa bulletin backlogs. Some applicants hold dual sponsorships to maximize options.

What This Means for You

If Your Priority Date Is Earlier Than September 15, 2013 (EB-2)

You may now be eligible to file Form I-485 with USCIS. This is a major milestone—you can apply for adjustment of status, receive an Employment Authorization Document (EAD), and begin the final stage of your green card journey. USCIS processing times typically range from 12–24 months after Form I-485 filing, depending on your field office location.

If Your Priority Date Falls Between September 15, 2013 and September 15, 2014

Your green card approval is likely 3–12 months away. Continue monitoring the monthly visa bulletin for updates. If your employer is prepared, you can request Form I-140 approval (if not already approved) and prepare I-485 documents in advance.

If Your Priority Date Is After September 15, 2014

You will likely wait several more years. During this wait, you can remain on H1B status with unlimited visa renewals. Explore AC21 job changes to accelerate your timeline if your new employer is willing to sponsor at an earlier priority date position.

USCIS Processing Time: What to Expect in 2026

The March 2026 visa bulletin advance also reflects shifting USCIS processing capacity. Current estimates for Form I-485 processing are:

  • USCIS Service Centers: 18–24 months average
  • Field Offices (Adjustment of Status): 12–18 months
  • Consular Processing (Abroad): 6–12 months after visa interview
  • EAD/AP Card Processing: 4–6 months from submission

Processing times vary significantly by location. Check the official USCIS processing times tool to estimate your specific timeline.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if my priority date is current but I'm still on OPT STEM extension?

You can file Form I-485 while on valid OPT status. In fact, filing I-485 protects your OPT employment authorization. Once I-485 is pending, you can switch jobs without filing a new OPT request. However, ensure your OPT end date extends beyond your expected I-485 decision date. If it doesn't, request a new OPT STEM extension before filing.

Can I change jobs if my EB-2 priority date is current but my green card hasn't been approved yet?

Yes, under AC21 (Section 245(c) portability). If your EB-2 I-140 petition has been pending for 180+ days, you can change employers without restarting your green card application. However, your new job must be in the same or closely related field and at the same or higher priority date level. Consult an immigration attorney to confirm your specific situation.

How often does the visa bulletin update, and when should I check it?

The U.S. Department of State releases the Visa Bulletin on the 15th of each month for the following month's availability.
