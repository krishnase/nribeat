April 2026 Visa Bulletin: EB-2 India Final Action Date Jumps +304 Days to July 15, 2014

📅 March 24, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ NRIBeat Editorial Source: travel.state.gov ↗

The U.S. Department of State has released the April 2026 Visa Bulletin with significant news for Indian green card applicants. The EB-2 India Final Action Date makes a large jump of +304 days — advancing from September 15, 2013 to July 15, 2014. EB-3 India Final Action Date holds at November 15, 2013 with no movement. USCIS has authorized the Dates for Filing chart this month, allowing eligible applicants to file I-485 / EAD / Advance Parole using the earlier DFF dates.

Table A — Final Action Dates (FAD)

The Final Action Date is the hard cutoff for green card approval. Your I-485 can only be approved once your priority date is on or before this date. This table always applies — USCIS does not need to authorize it.

The Dates for Filing is a separate, earlier date that lets you file your I-485, EAD (work permit), and Advance Parole before the Final Action Date is reached. USCIS must authorize this chart each month — it is not automatic. USCIS has authorized the DFF chart for April 2026.

What the April 2026 Movement Means for You

The +304 day jump in EB-2 India Final Action Date is unusually large — moving from September 15, 2013 to July 15, 2014 in one bulletin. This reflects additional visa number availability being released mid-fiscal year. It does not mean this pace will continue every month; normal monthly movement is 14–20 days.

If your EB-2 India priority date is between September 2013 and July 2014, you became current this month. Contact your immigration attorney immediately — visa numbers are limited each month and not guaranteed to roll over.

For EB-3 India, the Final Action Date held at November 15, 2013 with no movement. However, the DFF date moved to January 15, 2015, allowing more EB-3 applicants to file I-485 early and lock in EAD/AP protection.

Planning Around the Fiscal Year Cycle

The USCIS fiscal year runs October 1 – September 30. By late summer, annual India visa quotas often run out — triggering retrogression in August or September. The October bulletin then brings a large forward jump as fresh FY numbers are released. Mid-year jumps like April 2026's +304 day move happen when unused numbers from other countries get reallocated to oversubscribed countries like India.

EB-2 vs EB-3 India: Which Is Ahead?

For April 2026, EB-2 India Final Action (Jul 15, 2014) is ahead of EB-3 India (Nov 15, 2013) by approximately 8 months. On the DFF side, both EB-2 and EB-3 share the same filing date of January 15, 2015. If you hold an advanced degree, upgrading from EB-3 to EB-2 may be worth exploring — but consult your attorney, as category changes have implications for your I-140 and PERM.
