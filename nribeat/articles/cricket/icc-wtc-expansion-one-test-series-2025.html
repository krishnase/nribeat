ICC Expands WTC 2025: One-Test Series Now Allowed

📅 April 10, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ NRIBeat Editorial Source: ESPN Cricinfo ↗

The ICC is set to shake up Test cricket by allowing one-Test bilateral series to count toward the World Test Championship starting next cycle—and this could completely change how we track India cricket live scores and tournament standings.

What's Changed: WTC Expansion Explained

Here's the deal: An ICC working group led by Roger Twose has recommended that the board approve one-Test bilateral series as eligible for WTC points in the next competition cycle. Previously, only multi-Test series counted. This recommendation is headed to ICC board heads for final approval soon, and trust us—it's a bigger shift than it sounds.

The current World Test Championship structure has been pretty rigid: teams play longer series (usually 2-4 Tests) against each other, rack up points, and battle it out over two years for the trophy. Adding one-Test matches opens the door to more flexibility and potentially more international cricket for teams like India, who are juggling IPL 2025 schedule results alongside bilateral commitments.

For those of us following India cricket from the US, this means more matches to catch—whether early morning streams or late-night replays. More Tests = more India cricket live score updates on your phone at 6 AM EST.

Why the ICC Made This Move

Let's be real: Test cricket has been struggling for calendar space. Teams are packed with:

  • Bilateral Test series (2-4 matches)
  • ICC Champions Trophy 2025 and other major tournaments
  • IPL 2025 schedule results (franchise cricket that eats up weeks)
  • Domestic cricket commitments
  • Rest and rotation windows

By allowing single-Test matches to count toward WTC points, the ICC is trying to:

  • Increase Test cricket fixtures without demanding longer bilateral series
  • Create more scheduling flexibility for cricket boards managing packed calendars
  • Boost viewership by spreading Tests across more bilateral windows
  • Keep WTC competitive with more nations having realistic chances to rack up points

For India specifically, this could mean faster turnarounds between series and more opportunities to play meaningful Test cricket outside the traditional home-and-away format.

What This Means for India Cricket Fans in the US

If you're checking India cricket live score updates religiously at odd hours (guilty!), prepare yourself: there could be more Tests on the schedule. Here's what changes:

More Matches, Same Time Zones. India plays Tests across all five continents. One-Test series could pop up in unexpected windows—meaning surprise early-morning viewing opportunities (or late-night catches, depending on where India's playing).

Different Streaming Patterns. Unlike a full bilateral series, one-Test matches might get less traditional TV coverage in the US. But they'll still count for WTC points, so streaming services like Willow TV and Hotstar will likely pick them up.

Unpredictable Tournament Drama. With more matches spread across the cycle, the WTC standings could shift faster. Following India's progress becomes even more critical—one loss in a one-Test series hits harder than one loss in a 4-Test series.

The Road Ahead: ICC Champions Trophy 2025 & Beyond

The ICC Champions Trophy 2025 is still locked in as a 50-over format event, but this WTC change affects everything afterward. When the next Test cycle kicks off, expect the ICC to announce:

  • Updated bilateral schedules featuring one-Test series
  • New point structures (do one-Test matches award the same points as multi-Test series?)
  • Revised IPL 2025 schedule results coordination to avoid clashes
  • Clearer WTC qualification pathways for smaller cricket nations

India, as the world's top Test side (by rankings), could benefit from this format's flexibility. Fewer forced four-Test series means less fatigue for stars like Virat Kohli, Rohit Sharma, and the fast-bowling contingent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the World Test Championship, and why does this change matter?

The WTC is cricket's ultimate Test series—teams rack up points over two years, and the top two face off in a final. Allowing one-Test matches to count means more fixtures, faster point accumulation, and a tighter race for the trophy. For India cricket fans, it means more matches to follow and analyze.

Will one-Test series replace longer bilateral series completely?

Unlikely. The ICC is adding one-Test series as an option, not replacing traditional multi-Test bilaterals. Expect a mix—some teams might play a one-Test + a three-Test series against each other over a two-year cycle, creating more variety in international cricket schedules.

How does this affect India cricket live score tracking and IPL 2025 schedule results coordination?

More WTC-eligible fixtures mean better calendar flexibility for the BCCI to balance IPL windows with Test cricket. You'll see more India cricket live score updates spread across the year without cramming a four-Test series into a tight window.
