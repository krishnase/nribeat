Turning Point: The Pitch Will Be Decisive

Here's what determines this series: the Dhaka pitch. In April, the Sher-e-Bangla Stadium wicket is notoriously unpredictable. It's hard, it's dry in patches, and it can produce unexpected turn and bounce. For a New Zealand team relying on young players unfamiliar with subcontinental cricket, this is where matches are won and lost.

Bangladesh's spinners—particularly Shakib and their other experienced tweakers—will have information from years of playing at home. New Zealand's bowlers will be learning on the job. If the pitch breaks up early, Bangladesh wins. If it stays true, New Zealand has a fighting chance. But given the circumstances, expect Bangladesh to dictate terms from day one.
