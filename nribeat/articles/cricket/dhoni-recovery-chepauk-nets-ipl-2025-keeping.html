What Dhoni's Net Session Tells Us

When a champion like Dhoni returns to the nets after injury, it's never just about hitting balls. It's about testing the body, assessing pain levels, and rebuilding confidence. The fact that he's facing throwdowns—controlled deliveries from support staff rather than full-pace bowlers—indicates he's in the early stages of recovery.

Here's what typically happens in injury rehabilitation for cricketers:

  • Phase 1: Gym work and flexibility (already completed)
  • Phase 2: Net sessions with throwdowns (where Dhoni is now)
  • Phase 3: Facing full-pace bowling in nets
  • Phase 4: Match practice and potentially middle-order batting
  • Phase 5: Full match fitness and wicketkeeping duties

The decision to skip wicketkeeping duties at nets suggests Dhoni may not be ready for the physical demands of the position yet. Keeping requires constant movement, explosive dives, and quick reflexes—all things that could aggravate a healing injury.
