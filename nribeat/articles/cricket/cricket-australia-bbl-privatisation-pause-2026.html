What's Cricket Australia Trying to Do?

Cricket Australia's original plan was straightforward: bring in private investors to take over Big Bash League franchises. The idea sounds good on paper—more money, professional management, global visibility. But state cricket associations in Australia have fought tooth and nail because they fear losing their traditional power base.

The situation is complicated by the fact that state boards generate significant revenue through Sheffield Shield cricket and regional tournaments. A full privatisation of the BBL without safeguards could destabilise that ecosystem.
