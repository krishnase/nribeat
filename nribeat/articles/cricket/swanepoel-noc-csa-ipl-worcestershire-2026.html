Frequently Asked Questions

What is an NOC in cricket, and why does CSA issue them?

An NOC (No Objection Certificate) is official permission from a player's national board to compete in overseas tournaments. CSA issues NOCs to manage player availability, prevent conflicts with national duties, and maintain control over contract negotiations. It's essentially a regulatory tool that boards use to protect their interests while allowing players to earn from international leagues like the IPL.

Can Beyers Swanepoel play in IPL 2025 now that he has his NOC?

Yes, Swanepoel is now eligible to participate in IPL 2025 matches and training camps. The NOC clearance removes the administrative block that prevented him from joining his franchise. However, any disciplinary action from CSA could still impact his long-term availability or international commitments, so he's not completely in the clear yet.

What happens if CSA's disciplinary action is severe for Swanepoel?

Depending on the severity, CSA could impose fines, suspend him from international cricket temporarily, or restrict future NOC approvals. The exact consequences will depend on what violation occurred. For now, the board has allowed him to play overseas while reserving the right to punish the underlying breach—a pragmatic approach that lets the player earn while maintaining authority.
