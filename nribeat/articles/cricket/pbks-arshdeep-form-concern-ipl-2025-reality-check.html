Match Summary: PBKS' First Real Test

After what looked like a smooth start to their campaign, PBKS faced their first defeat, and it wasn't just about one bad day—it was a wake-up call about how quickly fortunes can shift in the IPL 2025 schedule. The team that had been breezing through matches suddenly looked vulnerable, and the bowling unit that was supposed to be their backbone showed cracks when it mattered.

But what really stood out was his admission about the bowling's "slightly below-par" performance. In Twenty20 cricket, especially in a competition as intense as the IPL, bowling form is everything. One bad spell can cost you a game—and that's exactly what happened here.

The scorecard told the story of a team that couldn't quite execute when it counted. Opposition batters got away with shots that should've been dots, and the death bowling—usually PBKS' strength—looked pedestrian for stretches of the game.

Key Performers: Arshdeep Struggles, Others Shine

Here's where it gets interesting from an India cricket live score perspective. While some PBKS bowlers had decent outings, Arshdeep Singh's numbers didn't reflect the high standards he's set for himself in recent seasons. The left-arm pacer, who's been crucial to India's T20I plans and has shown promise in IPL cricket, looked off the boil.

The concerning part? This isn't a one-off bad day. Arshdeep's form has been drifting, and against quality opposition in the IPL 2025 season, you simply can't afford inconsistency in your death bowling.

Other bowlers had moments, but none could really stamp their authority on the match. That's the nature of the IPL—if your best players aren't firing, the whole unit feels it.

The Turning Point: Death Overs Collapse

Every match has one moment where the course changes. For PBKS this time, it was their handling of the death overs. The opposition skipper connected with a couple of big shots that would normally be dot balls, but the bowling was just a touch short on execution. Lines and lengths weren't quite there, and in T20 cricket, that's the difference between defending 165 and conceding 180.

When Arshdeep Singh was bowling, batters seemed to have answers. A slower ball was read. A yorker was either missed or driven. The confidence that usually exudes from his bowling just wasn't visible, and opposition batters sensed it.

Series Standing & What's Next
