Salman Khan's Maatrubhumi Delayed to June 2026 Release

📅 April 07, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ NRIBeat Editorial Source: Bollywood Hungama ↗
Disclosure: NRIBeat may earn a commission from links in this article at no extra cost to you. We only recommend services we believe in.

Salman Khan's much-anticipated war drama Maatrubhumi has officially been pushed from its May 2026 release window to June 2026. The film, directed by Apoorva Lakhia, was originally conceived as a retelling of the 2020 Galwan Valley clash between India and China, but underwent significant creative changes following diplomatic requests from India's Ministry of Defence. This marks the first collaboration between Salman Khan and director Apoorva Lakhia, and it's shaping up to be one of the biggest Indian movies streaming and theatrical releases of the summer. Here's everything you need to know about this delayed Bollywood release.

The Story / What to Expect

Maatrubhumi began production as Battle of Galwan, a war drama directly inspired by the real-world military conflict in the Galwan Valley. However, during the filming process, geopolitical sensitivities became apparent. The Ministry of Defence approached the filmmakers with concerns about depicting the actual clash, particularly regarding how it might affect current India-China relations and diplomatic equations.

Rather than abandon the project, Salman Khan and Apoorva Lakhia pivoted to a fictionalized approach. The film now takes inspiration from real events but crafts an original narrative that captures the spirit of patriotism and sacrifice without directly recreating the historical incident. This creative evolution actually gives the film more dramatic freedom while maintaining its core message about heroism and national pride.

What makes Maatrubhumi particularly exciting for Indian cinema fans is the fresh pairing of Salman Khan with Apoorva Lakhia, known for his gritty, intense storytelling. The director has previously worked on hard-hitting dramas that don't shy away from complex themes. Expect:

  • High-octane action sequences and battle choreography
  • A patriotic narrative centered on courage and sacrifice
  • Strong character development beyond typical war film tropes
  • Salman Khan in a serious, dramatic role suited to the war genre
  • A runtime and scope befitting a major summer blockbuster

The June release timing suggests a theatrical-first strategy, positioning Maatrubhumi as a big-screen experience rather than an immediate OTT play. However, based on typical Bollywood release patterns, it will likely hit Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, or Disney+ Hotstar within 4-6 weeks of theatrical release.

Currently, Maatrubhumi has not been officially acquired by any streaming platform for North American distribution. However, based on Salman Khan's previous theatrical releases, here's where to expect it:

Theatrical Release (June 2026): Major US cities with significant Indian diaspora populations—New York, Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago, and Houston—will have theatrical screenings. Check Fandango or local cinema chains for showtimes.

Worth Watching?

Verdict: Yes, if you're a Salman Khan fan or appreciate patriotic war dramas. While the creative pivot from a factual retelling might disappoint history buffs, it actually positions the film to tell a more compelling fictional story. The combination of Salman Khan's star power, Apoorva Lakhia's directorial vision, and the war drama genre creates genuine intrigue.
