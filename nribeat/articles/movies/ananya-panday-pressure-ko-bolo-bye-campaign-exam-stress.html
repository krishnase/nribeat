Ananya Panday's 'Pressure Ko Bolo Bye' Campaign Tackles Exam Stress in 2025

Bollywood actor Ananya Panday has partnered with Nick India to launch the 'Pressure Ko Bolo Bye' campaign, a family-first initiative designed to help households manage the mounting stress and expectations that come with exam season. The collaboration brings together entertainment and mental health awareness, recognizing that exams often take over Indian family life and create unhealthy pressure on students.

This initiative, rooted in So Positive by Ananya Panday—her established platform for mental health and positivity—aims to turn exam stress into manageable, playful moments for families across India and for the Indian diaspora watching from the US. As streaming platforms like Netflix and Disney+ Hotstar continue to dominate Indian content consumption, this campaign represents how Bollywood celebrities are using their platforms to address real-world issues beyond entertainment.

The Story: A Campaign That Speaks to Every Indian Family

Exam season in Indian households is notoriously intense. Parents expect top grades, students face peer pressure, and the entire family dynamic shifts around board exams and competitive entrance tests. Nick India, a channel known for child-friendly programming, recognized this widespread issue and partnered with Ananya Panday to create a relatable, lighthearted response.

The 'Pressure Ko Bolo Bye' campaign encourages families to acknowledge exam stress openly and release it through simple, playful activities. Rather than ignoring the pressure or letting it fester, the initiative promotes:

  • Open conversations between parents and children about stress and expectations
  • Fun, creative activities to decompress during exam preparation
  • Reframing exam pressure as a manageable challenge rather than an overwhelming burden
  • Mental health awareness tailored to Indian family structures
  • Support from a celebrity who genuinely advocates for youth well-being

Ananya Panday's involvement is particularly significant because she's been vocal about her own mental health journey and the pressures of growing up in the public eye. Her So Positive initiative has built credibility with Gen Z audiences, making her a trusted voice on topics beyond traditional Bollywood gossip and new movie releases.

What This Campaign Offers Indian Families in the US

For Indian diaspora families across America, this campaign hits home. Parents often impose higher academic expectations on their children while maintaining cultural values, creating a unique pressure dynamic. The 'Pressure Ko Bolo Bye' initiative provides:

  • Parental Guidance: Resources for mothers and fathers to recognize and support their children during exam stress
  • Student-Focused Content: Relatable videos and tips that speak directly to teens and pre-teens
  • Cultural Context: Solutions designed for Indian families, not generic Western self-help approaches
  • Entertainment Integration: Making mental health discussions fun and engaging, not clinical or uncomfortable
  • Free Accessibility: Available through Nick India's platform, making it accessible beyond paid streaming subscriptions

Where to Watch and Engage with This Campaign

The 'Pressure Ko Bolo Bye' campaign is primarily accessible through Nick India, the children's channel available via:

  • Disney+ Hotstar Premium (subscription required; approximately $5.99/month in the US)
  • Nick India's YouTube channel (free, ad-supported)
  • Nick India's official website (free access to campaign materials and resources)
  • Traditional cable/satellite TV subscriptions that include Nick India

The campaign also promotes So Positive by Ananya Panday, her mental health platform accessible across social media and dedicated apps, offering additional resources for families navigating exam pressure.
