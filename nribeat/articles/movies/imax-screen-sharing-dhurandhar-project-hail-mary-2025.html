Project Hail Mary is a high-budget space thriller that absolutely demands IMAX's massive screens and immersive sound for full impact. The film's visual spectacle—designed for premium format exhibition—loses significant appeal on standard screens. Sony Pictures has been vocally frustrated about the constraint, and their advocacy triggered industry-wide conversations about fair screen allocation.

Dhurandhar: The Revenge Round 2 represents Bollywood's heavyweight entry into the IMAX conversation. As a major Indian release, it has strong local backing and audience anticipation. The Super Mario Galaxy Movie joins the competition as a family-friendly animated feature that likewise benefits from IMAX's technical superiority. Global releases like this typically secure premium screen allocations, but availability remains tight.

For Indian diaspora fans in the US, this battle is largely irrelevant—you'll be streaming these films anyway. But it reflects broader industry tensions about screen economics, release scheduling, and the value of premium formats in a post-pandemic theatrical landscape.
