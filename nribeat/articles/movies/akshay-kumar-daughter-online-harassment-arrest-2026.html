## How to Keep Your Children Safe Online in 2026

Whether your kids are binge-watching shows on Netflix India originals, scrolling TikTok, or gaming with friends, protection requires active parenting:

Set Clear Boundaries: Establish screen time limits and monitor which apps and websites your teenager uses. Use parental control apps that track online activity without violating privacy.

Have Regular Check-Ins: Ask your children about their online friends, what they're watching (new Bollywood movies 2025 on streaming services, for example), and whether anyone has made them uncomfortable. Create a judgment-free environment where they feel safe reporting problems.

Teach Digital Literacy: Explain that predators often pose as peers, that images shared online can be saved/manipulated, and that legitimate companies never ask for passwords or personal details via message.

Know the Platforms: If your kids use Instagram, Discord, or Snapchat, understand their privacy settings. Social media literacy is as important as reading literacy in 2026.

## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is sextortion and how does it target teenagers?
A: Sextortion is a form of sexual extortion where predators threaten to share intimate images (often fabricated using deepfake technology) unless victims pay money or comply with demands. Teens are targeted because they're more likely to trust strangers online and may have limited understanding of image manipulation.

Q: How can Indian parents in the US monitor their children's online activity while respecting privacy?
A: Use transparent parental controls, maintain open communication about which apps and platforms your child uses, and establish clear rules about what content is appropriate. Apps like Google Family Link and Apple Screen Time offer monitoring without complete surveillance. The key is balance between protection and trust.

Q: What should I do if my child becomes a victim of online harassment or sextortion?
A: Don't panic or blame your child. Report immediately to the National Cybercrime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), your local cyber police unit, and the specific platform where the harassment occurred. In the US, contact the FBI's IC3 (ic3.gov). Preserve all evidence (screenshots, messages) and consider counseling to help your child process trauma.
