Ranbir Kapoor's Ramayana Streaming Deal: Producer Targets Rs. 1000 Crore OTT Bid

Producer Namit Malhotra has made a bold move in Indian cinema: he rejected a historic Rs. 700 crore OTT offer for the upcoming Ramayana film starring Ranbir Kapoor and Yash, and is now negotiating for an even more ambitious Rs. 1000 crore streaming deal. With production budgets soaring to Rs. 4000 crores for two films combined, the stakes have never been higher for an Indian mythological adaptation heading to the streaming world.

The teaser, which dropped just days ago, showcases the cinematic grandeur of the project—featuring Ranbir Kapoor as Lord Ram in a visually stunning reimagining of the classic epic. For Indian diaspora audiences in the US looking to catch the latest new Bollywood movies 2026, this is the most talked-about streaming acquisition of the year.

Where to Watch in the US: OTT Platform & Availability

As of April 2026, the streaming platform for Ramayana has not been officially confirmed. However, industry insiders suggest the film will likely land on one of these major platforms serving Indian diaspora audiences in the US:

  • Disney+ Hotstar Premium — The primary home for large-budget Indian films; costs $9.99/month or $99.99/year in the US
  • Netflix — Recently increased focus on blockbuster Indian originals; $6.99–$22.99/month depending on plan

Given the Rs. 1000 crore price tag and production scale, Disney+ Hotstar is the frontrunner, as it has historically secured major mythological adaptations and high-budget Bollywood releases for global Indian audiences.

Timeline: Theatrical release is expected in 2026, with OTT exclusivity arriving 4–6 weeks after box office run concludes—a standard window for premium Bollywood films.
