The Story / What to Expect

Sanam Bewafa (1991) is a romantic tragedy that defined Salman Khan's career in the early 1990s. The film stars Salman alongside Chandni, exploring themes of love, betrayal, and redemption through a deeply emotional narrative. The movie became a cult classic, remembered for its soulful music by composers A.R. Rahman and Kalyanji-Anandji, and iconic dialogues that have stayed with audiences for over three decades.

This re-release presents the perfect opportunity to experience the film on the big screen as it was meant to be seen — with pristine print restoration and theatrical audio. For those unfamiliar with the story, expect a classic 90s Bollywood romance with strong emotional beats, memorable chemistry between leads, and music that defined an era. While you won't find the high-octane action sequences or comedy-heavy plots of modern Hindi movies, Sanam Bewafa delivers genuine pathos and cinematic grandeur that has aged remarkably well.

The re-release also capitalizes on the current wave of nostalgia in Indian cinema. After Sanam Teri Kasam became the highest-grossing re-release in history and Tere Naam drew significant audience interest, studios recognize that diaspora audiences and Hindi film enthusiasts are eager to relive classics on the big screen rather than waiting for these films to debut on OTT platforms like Netflix or Prime Video.

Where to Watch in the US

Theatrical Release (April 10, 2026)

Sanam Bewafa will have a limited theatrical release in select multiplexes across the US, particularly in cities with significant Indian diaspora populations such as New Jersey, California, Texas, and Illinois. Check your local cinema chains for showtimes — most major multiplexes in Indian-heavy neighborhoods typically screen re-releases on opening weekends.

Ticket prices typically range from $12–$16 depending on your location and theater format (standard vs. premium screens).

Once the theatrical run concludes (usually 2–4 weeks for re-releases), Sanam Bewafa will likely debut on digital platforms. While the film's current OTT home isn't officially announced, Salman Khan films typically release on Prime Video (usually free with subscription: $14.99/month or $139/year) or Netflix ($6.99–$22.99/month depending on tier). Worth Watching?

Yes, if you're a Bollywood nostalgia fan or Salman Khan enthusiast.

Sanam Bewafa is absolutely worth the theatrical experience, especially for:

  • Salman Khan fans wanting to celebrate his iconic 90s work
  • Audiences who love classic Hindi cinema with emotional depth
  • Those seeking an alternative to new Bollywood movies heavy on action or comedy

However, if you prefer contemporary storytelling, fast-paced narratives, or the latest Hindi movies with modern production values, you might want to skip this and wait for newer releases on Netflix India originals or upcoming theatrical launches.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Sanam Bewafa available on Netflix or Prime Video in the US?

As of April 2026, Sanam Bewafa is not streaming on major US OTT platforms. It's exclusively in theaters for its April 10 re-release. Once the theatrical window closes (typically 3–4 weeks), it will likely appear on Prime Video or Netflix, but exact dates haven't been announced.

Why are Bollywood re-releases trending so much in 2025–2026?

Re-releases have become a smart business strategy because: (1) production costs are minimal since the film already exists, (2) nostalgia drives strong audiences among diaspora and older viewers, (3) theaters benefit from consistent content during seasonal gaps, and (4) digitally restored classics deliver theater-quality experiences that OTT can't match. Sanam Teri Kasam's record-breaking success proved audiences will pay for big-screen nostalgia.

Should I watch Sanam Bewafa in theaters or wait for it on OTT?

Theater is recommended for this 1991 classic — the restored print, theatrical sound design, and big-screen emotion elevate the experience significantly. However, if you can't access local screenings, waiting for the OTT release is reasonable. The film's emotional core translates well to home viewing, and you'll save on theater tickets ($14–$16 vs. streaming subscription cost).

Conclusion

Salman Khan's Sanam Bewafa arrives in US cinemas on April 10, 2026, tapping into the booming re-release trend that has captivated Indian diaspora audiences. This romantic classic offers a theatrical experience that new Bollywood movies on Netflix and Prime Video can't replicate — authentic 90s cinema, iconic music, and nostalgic storytelling on the big screen.

For NRIs seeking connection to Hindi cinema's golden era or longtime Salman Khan fans, this is a must-watch event. Check your local multiplexes for showtimes, and don't miss this limited window before the film eventually moves to streaming platforms.
