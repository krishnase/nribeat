The Bombay High Court has dismissed an appeal filed by actor Anita Advani seeking legal recognition of her relationship with late superstar Rajesh Khanna as a marriage. Justice Sharmila Deshmukh rejected Advani's appeal challenging an earlier 2017 ruling by a Dindoshi civil court that had dismissed her original suit on technical grounds. This significant legal ruling closes a lengthy chapter in Bollywood history involving one of Hindi cinema's most iconic leading men and reminds us why staying updated on Bollywood news matters, even when stories extend beyond Netflix India originals and new Bollywood movies 2025 releases.

The Story: Understanding the Legal Battle

Rajesh Khanna, who passed away in 2012, remains one of Bollywood's most celebrated actors—a true superstar of the 1970s and 1980s known for films like Anand and Kabhie Kabhie. Anita Advani's claim stemmed from her assertion that she had been in a long-term relationship with Khanna that constituted a marriage under Hindu law, even without formal ceremonies or registration.

The legal journey has been complex. Advani first filed her suit in the Dindoshi civil court, which dismissed it in 2017 on technical grounds. Rather than accepting that decision, she filed an appeal with the Bombay High Court, hoping for a reversal. However, Justice Sharmila Deshmukh upheld the lower court's dismissal, effectively ending Advani's legal bid for marital recognition.

While this case may seem removed from discussions about where to watch new Bollywood movies or Netflix India originals, it reflects the real-world complexities that sometimes inspire cinema and documentaries exploring Indian society and relationships.

What Happens Next?

With the Bombay High Court's dismissal, Anita Advani's legal options are significantly limited. She could potentially seek special leave to appeal to the Supreme Court of India, but such applications face extremely high bars for acceptance. For practical purposes, this ruling likely represents the end of her legal battle for recognition as Rajesh Khanna's spouse.

The case closes without granting Advani the legal status she sought, preserving the legal boundaries around marital recognition under Indian law.
